Marine Life Contamination

Microplastics are commonly found in fish and shellfish along India's coastline. Over 60% of fish species studied near Mumbai were found to have microplastic contamination in 2020.

Microplastics in Drinking Water

Microplastic particles have been found in tap and bottled water in India, particularly in cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata.

Salt Contamination

A 2018 study revealed the presence of microplastics in Indian table salt from coastal and inland sources, raising concerns about microplastic consumption through daily salt intake.

Microplastics in Agriculture

Microplastics enter agricultural soils through fertilizers and plastic mulch, potentially contaminating crops. Further research is needed on the impact of microplastics in Indian farming systems.

Solutions and Government Response

The Indian government banned single-use plastics in 2022, but efforts are ongoing to improve waste management systems and remove microplastics from the environment.
